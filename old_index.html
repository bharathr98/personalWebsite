About Me

I am an aspiring mathematical/theoretical physicist in my fifth-year in the Integrated Masters (BS+MS) programme at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research Mohali and a visiting student at the International Centre for Theoretical Sciences at Bangalore.

I have a broad interest in high-energy physics and related areas. Currently, my focus mostly revolves around the field theoretic aspects of AdS/CFT, with an occasional dabbling in the stringy aspects of AdS/CFT, and even extensions to flat space holography.

Further information about my (past) work and my interests besides theoretical high-energy physics can be found below. Unfortunately, most of the interesting projects I am currently working on are at an early stage, but I hope to be able to write something on them soon.

Research Experience

Two dimensional holographic conformal field theories

Explored properties of Virasoro conformal blocks in semiclassical two-dimensional conformal field theories using bootstrap and monodromy methods, and its application in calculating holographic entanglement entropy. Currently, we are looking at situations involving entanglement wedge cross-section. Learn more.

The sign problem and the Lefschetz thimbles formalism

Explored applications of Picard-Lefschetz theory to the sign problem in non-perturbative QFT, with a focus on relation between Stokes phenomena and phase transitions in zero-dimensional bosonic models with quartic interactions. We are now looking to extend the results to situations in zero and higher number of dimensions, in the presence of supersymmetry. Learn more.

Entanglement in quantum field theory and gravity

Explored notions of entanglement in quantum field theory, focusing mainly on the Reeh-Schlieder theorem, the resulting algebra of local field operators and how correlations between operators in different spacetime regions arise. Other topics such as relative entropy of two states of a von Neumann algebra and its monotonicity, Tomita-Takesaki theory for finite-dimensional Hilbert spaces, and tradeoff between localisation of and entanglement between Rindler modes from the point of view of the AMPS firewall problem were also visited.

Computational investigation of cosmic microwave background data

Worked with other undergraduates on understanding the workings of the Horn reflector telescope used at Bell Laboratories, the methods of measurements by R.W. Wilson and A.A. Penzias, the methodologies used by D. J. Fixsen and J. C. Mather in their 1990 and 1994 papers on CMBR, development of programs for the analysis of data obtained from the Far Infrared Absolute Spectrophotometer (FIRAS) apparatus onboard the Cosmic Background Explorer (COBE) satellite and recreating the results presented in the 1994 paper by D. J. Fixsen et al.
